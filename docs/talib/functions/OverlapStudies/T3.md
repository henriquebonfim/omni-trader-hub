# T3 - Triple Exponential Moving Average (T3)

## Description

The T3 Moving Average, developed by Patrick Mulloy, is an advanced moving average that uses a unique smoothing technique to achieve very low lag while maintaining smoothness. Unlike the Triple Exponential Moving Average (TEMA), T3 uses a different mathematical approach that provides better smoothness with minimal lag.

## Category
Overlap Studies

## Author
Patrick Mulloy

## Calculation

T3 uses a unique smoothing approach with a volume factor (VF):

### Step 1: Calculate EMA Parameters
```
k = 2.0 / (period + 1.0)
one_minus_k = 1.0 - k
```

### Step 2: Calculate Three Levels of GD (Generalized DEMA)
```
EMA1 = EMA(Price, period)
EMA2 = EMA(EMA1, period)
GD1 = EMA1 * (1 + vFactor) - EMA2 * vFactor

EMA3 = EMA(GD1, period)
EMA4 = EMA(EMA3, period)
GD2 = EMA3 * (1 + vFactor) - EMA4 * vFactor

EMA5 = EMA(GD2, period)
EMA6 = EMA(EMA5, period)
GD3 = EMA5 * (1 + vFactor) - EMA6 * vFactor
```

### Step 3: Final T3
```
T3 = GD3
```

**Note**: T3 uses three levels of GD (Generalized DEMA) operations, each applying the volume factor to reduce lag while maintaining smoothness.

## Parameters

- **optInTimePeriod** (default: 5): Period for EMAs
  - Valid range: 2 to 100000
  - Common values: 5, 8, 10, 21

- **optInVFactor** (default: 0.7): Volume factor (0-1)
  - Valid range: 0 to 1
  - Common values: 0.5, 0.7, 0.8

## Inputs
- Price data: `double[]` (typically closing prices)

## Outputs
- T3 values: `double[]`

## Interpretation

### Characteristics
- **Very Low Lag**: Minimal lag compared to other MAs
- **Smooth**: Maintains smoothness despite low lag
- **Responsive**: Reacts quickly to price changes
- **Advanced**: Most sophisticated MA available

### Usage
1. **Trend Identification**:
   - Price above T3 = Uptrend
   - Price below T3 = Downtrend
   - T3 slope shows trend strength

2. **Entry/Exit Signals**:
   - Price crossing T3 generates signals
   - Very early signals due to low lag
   - May require confirmation

3. **Dynamic Support/Resistance**:
   - T3 acts as very responsive support/resistance
   - Excellent for trailing stops
   - Good for entry timing

## Implementation Details

The TA-Lib T3 implementation:

1. **Six EMAs**: Calculates six levels of EMA smoothing
2. **Coefficient Calculation**: Applies volume factor to derive coefficients
3. **Weighted Combination**: Combines EMAs with calculated weights
4. **Volume Factor**: Controls responsiveness vs. smoothness
5. **Lookback**: Requires 6×period bars for first output

## Trading Strategies

### 1. Pure T3 Strategy
- **Buy**: Price crosses above T3
- **Sell**: Price crosses below T3
- **Stop**: Below/above T3
- **Best in**: Trending markets

### 2. T3 + Confirmation
- **Setup**: T3 for direction
- **Confirmation**: RSI, MACD, or volume
- **Entry**: When both align
- **Exit**: T3 reversal or confirmation loss
- **Best in**: Reducing false signals

### 3. Multiple T3 System
- **Setup**: Two T3s (e.g., 5 and 21 period)
- **Buy**: Fast T3 crosses above slow T3
- **Sell**: Fast T3 crosses below slow T3
- **Best in**: Trend change detection

### 4. T3 + Price Action
- **Context**: T3 for trend
- **Entry**: Price patterns at T3 touches
- **Example**: Bull flag pullback to T3
- **Confirmation**: Candlestick patterns
- **Best in**: Combining technical analysis

## Volume Factor (VF) Settings

### Low VF (0.3-0.5)
- **Characteristics**: More smoothing, less responsive
- **Use**: Longer-term trading, less noise
- **Trade-off**: More lag, fewer whipsaws
- **Best for**: Position trading

### Medium VF (0.6-0.8)
- **Characteristics**: Balanced smoothness and responsiveness
- **Use**: General trading, most common
- **Trade-off**: Good balance
- **Best for**: Swing trading

### High VF (0.8-1.0)
- **Characteristics**: More responsive, less smooth
- **Use**: Short-term trading, quick signals
- **Trade-off**: More whipsaws, less lag
- **Best for**: Day trading

## Additional Notes

Patrick Mulloy developed T3 as the ultimate moving average, combining minimal lag with smoothness. The T3 represents the state-of-the-art in moving average technology.

### Key Insights

1. **Volume Factor Control**:
   - VF = 0: Pure smoothing (like SMA)
   - VF = 1: Maximum responsiveness (like price)
   - VF = 0.7: Balanced approach (default)
   - Adjust based on market conditions

2. **Six-Level Smoothing**:
   - Uses six EMAs for maximum smoothing control
   - Each level adds smoothing but reduces lag
   - Final combination optimizes both characteristics
   - More sophisticated than DEMA/TEMA

3. **Best Applications**:
   - Day trading (low lag needed)
   - Scalping (very responsive)
   - Trend following (early signals)
   - Volatile markets (adapts to price changes)

4. **Parameter Selection**:
   - Period: 5-10 for day trading, 20-30 for swing trading
   - VF: 0.5-0.7 for balanced, 0.8+ for very responsive
   - Test different combinations for your market
   - Higher VF = more signals but more noise

5. **Combination Strategies**:
   - Use T3 for trend direction
   - Use oscillators for overbought/oversold
   - Use volume for confirmation
   - Use multiple timeframes for context

### Practical Tips

**For Day Trading**:
- Use short periods (5-8)
- Higher VF (0.7-0.9)
- More responsive to price changes
- Good for quick entries/exits

**For Swing Trading**:
- Use medium periods (10-21)
- Medium VF (0.6-0.8)
- Balanced approach
- Good for trend following

**For Position Trading**:
- Use longer periods (20-50)
- Lower VF (0.5-0.7)
- More smoothing, less noise
- Good for major trends

**Avoiding Whipsaws**:
- Use T3 with trend filter (ADX)
- Require confirmation from other indicators
- Use multiple timeframes
- Avoid in very choppy markets

T3 is the most advanced moving average available and is particularly valuable for experienced traders who need the fastest possible signals while maintaining reasonable smoothness. It's ideal for day trading and short-term strategies where lag is critical.
